Special : 31 Years of Back to the Future

Back to the Future is one of the best movies of the 80's and is still a very watched movie today, largely because of its theories with the times we live. Robert Zemeckis (film producer) brings us one of the funniest films in cinema, of course, brushes up on physics, but takes it all in fun, it's a film without a big message but that's not why it's empty, like a cinematographic work It has everything: Screenplay, Soundtrack, Photography, editing, mixing. Starting with the screenplay that deals with time travel in an extremely simple, light and fun way, and oddly enough, it is complete, leaves nothing to be desired, everything is very well resolved and the characters are all very well developed, besides leaving that good hook in a natural way. Another big point is Alan Silvestre's soundtrack, it's epic and dictates the sound of the movie, and all the other soundtracks in the movie are unique, completely 80s, just like the grayish photography. Next, we will make a comparison between the films in the trilogy.

Back to the future I:

Marty McFly, a teenager from a small town in California, is transported to the 1950s when eccentric scientist Doc Brown's experiment goes awry. Traveling through time in a modified car, the Delorean, Marty meets young versions of his parents and needs to make them fall in love, or else he will cease to exist. To complicate matters, Marty needs to get home in time to save the scientist.

Back to the Future II:

Marty McFly and scientist Doc Brown travel from 1985 to 2015 to prevent Marty's son from ruining the McFly family's future. However, his archenemy Biff Tannen steals Doc's time machine, the DeLorean, and uses it to alter history to their advantage, forcing the pair to return to 1955 to restore the timeline.
